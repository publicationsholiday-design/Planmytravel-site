Washington DC rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Washington DC rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Monuments, Memorials & Museum Grandeur

09:00 AM

National Mall Exploration: Lincoln Memorial & Reflecting Pool

Begin your DC journey with an awe-inspiring visit to the Lincoln Memorial, standing majestically at the west end of the National Mall. Reflect on history as you gaze upon the iconic statue and the vast Reflecting Pool, a symbol of national unity. Ascend the steps for panoramic views of the Washington Monument and Capitol Building.

10:30 AM

World War II Memorial & Washington Monument

Stroll eastward along the National Mall to the poignant World War II Memorial, honoring those who served. Continue your walk to admire the towering Washington Monument, an enduring obelisk that dominates the city's skyline. While ascents require tickets, its presence is magnificent from every angle.

12:30 PM

Smithsonian National Museum of American History

Immerse yourself in the narrative of the American experience at this unparalleled museum. Discover iconic artifacts like the Star-Spangled Banner, Dorothy's Ruby Slippers, and the First Ladies' gowns. Spend ample time exploring the diverse exhibits that chronicle the nation's journey.

03:30 PM

White House Exterior & Lafayette Square

Head north from the Mall to get a glimpse of the iconic White House from Lafayette Square. Observe the executive mansion, the heart of American democracy, and stroll through the historic park, often a site of peaceful protests and public gatherings. It's a quintessential DC photo opportunity.

07:00 PM

Dinner in Penn Quarter & Illuminated Monuments Tour

Enjoy an upscale dinner in Penn Quarter, a lively district known for its diverse culinary scene and proximity to cultural venues. Afterward, consider an evening stroll or a guided tour to see the major monuments beautifully illuminated against the night sky, offering a truly magical perspective.

Day 2 Capitol Hill, Culture & Historic Charm

09:00 AM

U.S. Capitol Building (Exterior & Visitor Center)

Start your day on Capitol Hill with an impressive view of the U.S. Capitol Building, the seat of the legislative branch. Explore the exterior and consider a visit to the Capitol Visitor Center to learn about its history and function. Pre-booking tours is highly recommended for interior access.

11:00 AM

Library of Congress & Supreme Court

Marvel at the stunning architecture and rich collections of the Library of Congress, especially the Thomas Jefferson Building, renowned for its intricate details and grand reading room. Just a short walk away, view the impressive facade of the Supreme Court, the highest judicial body in the U.S.

01:00 PM

United States Botanic Garden & Lunch in Capitol Hill

Escape into the lush tranquility of the United States Botanic Garden, a living plant museum displaying diverse ecosystems. Afterwards, enjoy a delightful lunch at one of the many charming cafes or bistros scattered throughout the Capitol Hill neighborhood, favored by locals.

03:30 PM

National Portrait Gallery or Smithsonian American Art Museum

Indulge in art and history at the shared building of the National Portrait Gallery and the Smithsonian American Art Museum. Discover portraits of influential Americans or explore the rich tapestry of American art from colonial times to the present. The building itself is a masterpiece.

07:00 PM

Dinner & Evening Stroll in Georgetown

Conclude your day with an exquisite dinner in the historic and elegant neighborhood of Georgetown. This charming area boasts cobblestone streets, high-end boutiques, and a plethora of fine dining options. After dinner, enjoy a leisurely stroll past its beautiful colonial-era homes and waterfront.

Day 3 Diplomacy, Innovation & Local Flavors

09:00 AM

International Spy Museum

Dive into the clandestine world of espionage at the thrilling International Spy Museum. Explore a vast collection of spy gadgets, learn about famous intelligence operations, and even try your hand at undercover missions. It's an interactive experience for all ages.

11:30 AM

Tidal Basin: Jefferson & MLK Jr. Memorials

Experience the serenity of the Tidal Basin. Visit the grand Thomas Jefferson Memorial, offering stunning views across the water, and then reflect at the powerful Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial, carved from the 'Stone of Hope.' A serene and thought-provoking area.

01:30 PM

The Wharf for Lunch & Waterfront Experience

Head to The Wharf, DC's vibrant waterfront district, for a dynamic lunch experience. Choose from a range of casual to upscale eateries, enjoy the lively atmosphere, and take in the stunning Potomac River views. You can also explore the historic Municipal Fish Market here.

03:30 PM

National Air and Space Museum

Delve into humanity's quest for flight and space exploration at the renowned National Air and Space Museum. Marvel at iconic aircraft like the Wright Flyer and the Apollo 11 Command Module. It's a journey through innovation and adventure (check for renovation updates on specific halls).

07:00 PM

Farewell Dinner in Logan Circle or Shaw

Enjoy a farewell dinner in one of DC's most dynamic neighborhoods, Logan Circle or Shaw. These areas are celebrated for their culinary innovation, from farm-to-table bistros to trendy eateries. Experience the energetic local scene and savor the diverse flavors that make DC a premier food destination.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Washington DC works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: For budget-conscious travelers, consider areas like Adams Morgan or Columbia Heights, which offer more affordable guesthouses and direct Metro access to downtown, providing a vibrant local experience.
Mid-range: Trendy, central districts perfect for mid-tier boutique stays include Logan Circle, Dupont Circle, and Capitol Hill. These areas boast charming rowhouses converted into hotels, excellent dining, and cultural vibrancy, with superb walkability and Metro connectivity.
Luxury: Elite, ultra-luxury zones suited for 5-star premier travel include Georgetown, the West End, and the area surrounding Lafayette Square. These neighborhoods offer top-tier hotel brands, exclusive amenities, and proximity to diplomatic and governmental centers, ensuring unparalleled service and elegance.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: The WMATA Metro system, often called 'the Metro,' is the cheapest and most efficient public transport. Purchase a SmarTrip card for seamless travel across the city's extensive subway and bus network. The DC Circulator bus also provides affordable, convenient loops through popular tourist areas.
Mid-range: A balanced approach mixes Metro travel for longer distances with standard ridesharing services (Uber/Lyft) for shortcuts, particularly for shorter hops or when time is of the essence. This offers flexibility and reduces walking, while still being cost-effective compared to exclusive private transport.
Luxury: For premium luxury transfers, opt for private car services, black car chauffeured methods, or dedicated executive sedans. These can be pre-booked for airport transfers, point-to-point travel, or for a full day of sightseeing, offering privacy, comfort, and personalized service.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Top food stalls and markets famous for cheap culinary excellence can be found in ethnic enclaves like Columbia Heights (Latin American cuisine), Adams Morgan (diverse global eats), and the U Street Corridor (historic 'Black Broadway' with great half-smokes and Ethiopian fare). Food trucks near the National Mall offer quick, diverse, and affordable lunch options.
Mid-range: Vibrant bistro alleys and popular dining spots are concentrated in Logan Circle, Dupont Circle, and the 14th Street Corridor. Capitol Hill also offers numerous charming restaurants and cafes popular with locals and political staffers. Shaw is known for its inventive kitchens and lively bar scene.
Luxury: Exclusive reservation districts for fine dining and Michelin-tier experiences include parts of Penn Quarter, Georgetown, and the West End. These areas house some of the city's most celebrated chefs and offer exquisite culinary journeys in sophisticated settings. Reservations are highly recommended.
