3 Days in Munich: A Complete Day-by-Day Itinerary

Exactly how to spend a perfect 3-day trip to Munich — what to see, where to stay, and how to get around.

Duration
3 Days
Best for
First-timers

Munich rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Munich rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Royal Grandeur & Historic Core Exploration

09:00 AM

Marienplatz & Glockenspiel Spectacle

Begin your Munich immersion at the vibrant Marienplatz, the city's main square. Witness the captivating Glockenspiel chime and dance at 11 AM (and noon, 5 PM in summer) at the New Town Hall (Neues Rathaus), a neo-Gothic masterpiece. Soak in the atmosphere and admire the architecture.

11:30 AM

Frauenkirche Ascent & Panoramic Views

Walk to the iconic Frauenkirche (Church of Our Lady), an emblematic symbol of Munich. Explore its grand interior and, if open, ascend one of its twin towers for breathtaking panoramic views across the city rooftops and towards the Alps on a clear day.

01:00 PM

Upscale Bavarian Lunch Experience

Indulge in a premium Bavarian lunch at a traditional, yet refined 'Gasthaus' near Marienplatz. Savor authentic dishes like roast pork with crackling or traditional sausages, paired with local beer, in an elegant setting that combines historic charm with excellent service.

02:30 PM

Munich Residenz & Hofgarten Serenity

Step into the opulent world of the Wittelsbach monarchs at the Munich Residenz, a sprawling palace complex. Explore its lavish rooms, magnificent courtyards, and the treasury. Afterward, find tranquility in the adjacent Hofgarten, a beautiful Renaissance garden perfect for a leisurely stroll.

07:00 PM

Elegant Dinner in Lehel

Conclude your day with an exquisite dinner in the upscale Lehel district, known for its refined dining establishments. Choose from a selection of modern European or sophisticated Bavarian cuisine, enjoying impeccable service in an intimate setting.

Day 2 Art, Culture & Green Escapes

09:30 AM

Kunstareal & Ancient Greek Treasures

Immerse yourself in art at the Kunstareal, Munich's museum quarter. Focus on the Glyptothek, a museum dedicated to ancient Greek and Roman sculpture. Admire its classical architecture and serene courtyards before delving into its impressive collection.

11:30 AM

Old Masters or Modern Art Exploration

Continue your cultural journey by choosing between two world-class art institutions: the Alte Pinakothek, showcasing European masters from the 14th to 18th centuries, or the Lenbachhaus, known for its stunning collection of Blue Rider art and contemporary works. Both offer profound artistic experiences.

01:30 PM

Maxvorstadt Bistro Lunch

Enjoy a sophisticated lunch in Maxvorstadt, the vibrant district surrounding the Kunstareal. This area is known for its chic cafes, lively bistros, and diverse culinary offerings, perfect for a refined midday meal amidst intellectual surroundings.

03:00 PM

English Garden Serenity & Surfing

Escape to the vast English Garden (Englischer Garten), one of the world's largest urban parks. Witness the famous Eisbach wave surfers, stroll along serene paths to the Monopteros for city views, or relax at the iconic Chinese Tower Beer Garden for a traditional Bavarian 'Maß' of beer and pretzels.

07:30 PM

Modern Bavarian Dining Experience

Indulge in a contemporary Bavarian dinner. Opt for a restaurant that reinterprets traditional dishes with a modern twist, using fresh, regional ingredients. Explore a restaurant in Glockenbachviertel or Haidhausen for a lively yet refined ambiance, perhaps paired with excellent local wines or craft beers.

Day 3 Palatial Splendor & Munich's Innovation

10:00 AM

Nymphenburg Palace & Gardens

Journey to the magnificent Nymphenburg Palace, the former summer residence of Bavarian rulers. Explore its lavish interiors, including the 'Gallery of Beauties,' and wander through its expansive baroque gardens, discovering smaller palaces like the Amalienburg and Badenburg.

01:00 PM

Lunch near Nymphenburg

Enjoy a leisurely lunch at a charming restaurant or café conveniently located near Nymphenburg Palace, offering local specialties or light, modern fare. This provides a relaxing break before your afternoon explorations.

02:30 PM

BMW Welt & Museum Exploration

Dive into the world of automotive excellence at BMW Welt, a stunning architectural marvel showcasing the latest vehicles, and the adjacent BMW Museum, which traces the company's fascinating history from its beginnings to futuristic concepts. A must for enthusiasts of design and engineering.

06:00 PM

Viktualienmarkt Delights & Souvenirs

Return to the city center and spend time at the bustling Viktualienmarkt. Even in the late afternoon, it's a sensory delight. Browse gourmet food stalls, perhaps enjoy a final local snack or drink, and pick up some high-quality culinary souvenirs or artisanal crafts.

07:30 PM

Farewell Fine Dining in Schwabing

Savor a memorable farewell dinner in the sophisticated Schwabing district, known for its elegant restaurants and vibrant atmosphere. Choose a venue offering innovative German cuisine or an international gastronomic experience to toast to your extraordinary Munich adventure.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Munich works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: Haidhausen (eastern parts), Sendling, or districts slightly outside the Altstadt well-connected by public transport for excellent value.
Mid-range: Maxvorstadt, Schwabing (southern areas), or Glockenbachviertel for vibrant, design-focused boutique hotels.
Luxury: Altstadt (around Marienplatz/Odeonsplatz), Lehel, or Bogenhausen for unparalleled luxury, impeccable service, and prestigious addresses.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: Munich's efficient MVV public transport system (U-Bahn, S-Bahn, Tram, Bus) with a 3-day 'Innenraum' (inner district) or 'Gesamtnetz' (entire network) ticket.
Mid-range: Utilizing the MVV network for core travel supplemented by reputable local taxi services or ridesharing apps for evening and less accessible routes.
Luxury: Premium chauffeured services, black car transfers, or dedicated hotel car services for seamless, private, and door-to-door transportation.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Viktualienmarkt for diverse and affordable stalls, numerous local bakeries ('Bäckereien') for breakfast and snacks, and traditional 'Wirtshäuser' offering hearty Bavarian fare at reasonable prices in districts like Au-Haidhausen.
Mid-range: Explore the trendy bistros and traditional Bavarian eateries in Glockenbachviertel, the chic cafes and international cuisine of Maxvorstadt, or the bustling dining scene of Schwabing.
Luxury: The sophisticated dining rooms in Altstadt, Lehel, and Bogenhausen districts, home to Munich's Michelin-starred establishments and exclusive gourmet experiences.
