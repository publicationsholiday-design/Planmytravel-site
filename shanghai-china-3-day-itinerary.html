3 Days in Shanghai: A Complete Day-by-Day Itinerary

Exactly how to spend a perfect 3-day trip to Shanghai — what to see, where to stay, and how to get around.

Shanghai rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Shanghai rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Historic Waterfronts & European Elegance

09:00 AM

Stroll Along The Bund

Begin your Shanghai journey with a captivating walk along The Bund, admiring the majestic colonial-era architecture that lines the Huangpu River. Witness the stark contrast with Pudong's futuristic skyline across the water. This iconic promenade offers unparalleled photo opportunities.

11:00 AM

Explore Yu Garden & Old City God Temple Area

Delve into the heart of old Shanghai. Wander through the exquisite classical Chinese gardens of Yu Garden, a serene oasis amidst the bustling Old City. Afterward, explore the vibrant surrounding market streets and the historic City God Temple, sampling local snacks and soaking in the traditional atmosphere.

01:00 PM

Traditional Shanghai Lunch

Enjoy an authentic Shanghai lunch in the Old City, perhaps sampling xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) or other local delicacies from a highly-regarded, non-touristy eatery tucked away on a side street.

03:00 PM

Wander the French Concession

Immerse yourself in the tree-lined avenues and historic villas of the former French Concession. Explore streets like Wukang Road and Fuxing Road, admiring the unique blend of Art Deco architecture and charming boutiques. Discover hidden cafes and art galleries.

07:00 PM

Dinner & Rooftop Cocktails

Indulge in a sophisticated dinner within the French Concession, choosing from a range of elegant restaurants. Conclude your day with a memorable cocktail at a rooftop bar overlooking The Bund, enjoying the dazzling night lights of both the historic and modern skylines.

Day 2 Futuristic Visions & Artistic Expressions

09:30 AM

Ascend a Pudong Skyscraper

Cross over to the Pudong district and ascend one of its iconic skyscrapers, such as the Shanghai Tower or the Oriental Pearl TV Tower. Experience breathtaking panoramic views of the city, truly appreciating Shanghai's rapid development and architectural prowess from above.

11:30 AM

Explore Lujiazui Financial District

Wander through the futuristic landscape of Lujiazui. Visit the Shanghai Urban Planning Exhibition Center (if open) to understand the city's incredible growth, or explore the modern Pudong Art Museum.

01:00 PM

Upscale Lunch in Lujiazui

Enjoy a sophisticated lunch within one of Lujiazui's high-end shopping malls (like IFC Mall) or a restaurant offering views, sampling contemporary Chinese or international cuisine.

03:00 PM

Discover M50 Creative Park

Journey to M50 Creative Park, a vibrant contemporary art district housed in former industrial buildings. Browse numerous galleries, studios, and art spaces showcasing cutting-edge Chinese and international artwork. It's a fantastic spot for art lovers and unique souvenirs.

07:00 PM

Jing'an District Dinner & Temple Night View

Head to the Jing'an District for dinner, known for its mix of modern establishments and traditional charm. After your meal, witness the illuminated Jing'an Temple, a stunning contrast of ancient Buddhist architecture against a backdrop of modern skyscrapers.

Day 3 Cultural Depths & Local Lifestyle

09:30 AM

Immerse in Shanghai Museum

Spend your morning at the Shanghai Museum in People's Square, one of China's finest museums. Explore its extensive collections of ancient Chinese art, bronzes, ceramics, calligraphy, and more, offering deep insights into thousands of years of Chinese culture.

11:30 AM

Observe Local Life in People's Park

Step into People's Park, a vibrant green space in the heart of the city. Witness local life unfold, from tai chi practitioners and dancers to the famous 'marriage market' where parents seek suitable partners for their adult children. A truly unique cultural experience.

01:00 PM

Stylish Lunch in Xintiandi

Enjoy a leisurely lunch in Xintiandi, a car-free area known for its reconstructed traditional 'shikumen' (stone-gate houses) that now house upscale restaurants, cafes, and boutiques. It's a blend of old Shanghai charm and modern sophistication.

03:00 PM

Wander Tianzifang Art Lanes

Explore Tianzifang, a labyrinthine maze of narrow alleyways filled with art studios, quirky boutiques, craft shops, and charming cafes and bars. Similar to Xintiandi but with a more bohemian and less polished feel, it's perfect for discovering unique treasures.

06:30 PM

Huangpu River Cruise & Farewell Dinner

Conclude your Shanghai adventure with a spectacular evening Huangpu River cruise. Witness the city's iconic landmarks illuminated against the night sky, offering a truly magical perspective. Follow with a memorable farewell dinner at a premium restaurant, perhaps on The Bund or in the French Concession.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Shanghai works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: Consider areas around People's Square or the northern parts of Jing'an District for good value hotels with excellent subway access. Districts like Hongkou also offer more local options.
Mid-range: The charming French Concession (e.g., Xuhui District), the vibrant Xintiandi area, or the southern edges of The Bund offer boutique hotels and guesthouses with character and central access.
Luxury: The iconic Bund waterfront, the futuristic Lujiazui financial district (Pudong), or the prestigious Jing'an District (around the temple) are home to Shanghai's most opulent 5-star hotels with world-class amenities and breathtaking views.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: The Shanghai Metro system is extensive, efficient, and very affordable, covering almost all major attractions. Public buses are also an option for shorter distances and local immersion.
Mid-range: Utilize the Shanghai Metro for long distances, supplementing with local ride-hailing services (e.g., Didi) or standard metered taxis for convenience, especially within central districts or late evenings.
Luxury: Opt for private chauffeured cars booked through concierge services or premium ride-hailing options. For airport transfers, dedicated luxury limousine services offer seamless and comfortable transit.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Explore local eateries and street food stalls within the Old City (Nanshi) and areas around People's Square. Markets like Wujiang Road Food Street (though evolving) or local spots in residential neighborhoods offer authentic and cheap eats.
Mid-range: The French Concession boasts a myriad of charming bistros, local noodle shops, and trendy cafes. Xintiandi offers a blend of international and local cuisine in a stylish setting. The Jing'an Temple area also has a vibrant mid-range dining scene.
Luxury: The Bund is famous for its collection of high-end restaurants, many with stunning skyline views. Lujiazui's skyscraper dining offers unparalleled panoramic experiences. The French Concession also hosts numerous Michelin-starred establishments and exclusive private kitchens.
