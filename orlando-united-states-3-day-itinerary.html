Orlando rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Orlando rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Iconic Magic & Gastronomic Excellence

08:00 AM

Early Magic at the Iconic Kingdom

Experience the enchantment of Cinderella Castle and beloved attractions with a strategic early arrival to Magic Kingdom. Consider a VIP tour or rope drop strategy for an enhanced, crowd-minimized experience.

01:00 PM

Refined Bites within a Premier Resort

Savor a delightful lunch at a high-quality table-service restaurant within one of the Magic Kingdom area's premier resorts, offering a temporary escape from the park bustle and a sophisticated dining experience.

03:00 PM

Lakeside Luxury at Disney Springs

Transition to Disney Springs for an afternoon of upscale shopping, exploring unique boutiques, and enjoying the vibrant lakeside atmosphere. Indulge in artisanal treats or a sophisticated cocktail at a waterside lounge.

07:00 PM

Culinary Journey: A Michelin-Tier Experience

Conclude your day with an exquisite fine dining experience at one of Orlando's top culinary destinations, possibly a Michelin Guide-recognized establishment in Winter Park or a premier resort, offering a tasting menu with impeccable service.

Day 2 Thrills, Tranquility & Artistic Flair

09:00 AM

Immersive Adventures at Universal

Dive into the incredible worlds of Universal Studios or Islands of Adventure. Prioritize The Wizarding World of Harry Potter for unparalleled immersion, or experience thrilling rides with an Express Pass for efficiency.

01:30 PM

Trendy Bites at CityWalk

Enjoy a lively lunch at Universal CityWalk, offering a variety of popular, themed restaurants with vibrant atmospheres and diverse culinary options, perfect for a mid-day break from the parks.

03:30 PM

Winter Park Elegance & Lake Exploration

Escape to the charming streets of Winter Park. Stroll along Park Avenue, browsing art galleries and high-end boutiques, then embark on the scenic boat tour through the Chain of Lakes for stunning views of lakeside mansions and nature.

07:00 PM

Gastronomic Delights on Restaurant Row

Experience the vibrant dining scene of Dr. Phillips' 'Restaurant Row' on Sand Lake Road, featuring a concentration of Orlando's most acclaimed upscale eateries specializing in steakhouses, seafood, and international cuisine.

Day 3 Nature's Embrace, Relaxation & Culinary Finale

09:00 AM

Wild Florida Airboat Adventure

Embark on an exhilarating airboat ride through the Everglades ecosystem just outside Orlando, spotting alligators, birds, and other wildlife in their natural habitat, offering a unique glimpse into Florida's wild side.

01:00 PM

Fresh Flavors in a Modern Setting

Enjoy a light yet sophisticated farm-to-table lunch in the innovative Lake Nona district or a charming boutique cafe. Focus on fresh, locally sourced ingredients in a serene atmosphere.

03:00 PM

Pampering Retreat or Premier Shopping

Indulge in a relaxing spa treatment at one of Orlando's luxury resort spas, or enjoy a final round of high-end retail therapy at The Mall at Millenia, known for its premium brands and elegant ambiance.

06:30 PM

Farewell Dinner with a View

Conclude your Orlando journey with a memorable dinner at a restaurant offering stunning city skyline views, perhaps a rooftop experience in downtown Orlando or a sophisticated establishment with panoramic vistas from a premier resort.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Orlando works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: Budget-conscious travelers will find value in the southern stretches of International Drive, particularly around the I-Drive Premium Outlets area, and sections of Kissimmee near US-192, offering easy access to parks without the premium resort price.
Mid-range: For a blend of style and convenience, explore the northern part of International Drive, the Dr. Phillips district near Restaurant Row, or boutique properties in the vibrant Lake Eola Heights neighborhood near downtown. These areas offer upscale amenities and lively surroundings.
Luxury: Elite luxury is found within the exclusive resort enclaves, such as the areas surrounding Walt Disney World's flagship properties (e.g., Grand Floridian, Polynesian), Universal Orlando's premium hotels (e.g., Portofino Bay), or the modern, sophisticated Lake Nona community, known for its wellness and innovation focus.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: Utilize the LYNX public bus system for city-wide access and the I-RIDE Trolley for localized transit along International Drive. Walking within resort areas and designated entertainment complexes is also efficient.
Mid-range: Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft offer a balanced blend of convenience and cost for navigating between major attractions and neighborhoods. Many resort properties also offer shuttle services to their associated parks and entertainment districts.
Luxury: Opt for private car services, chauffeured black cars, or executive SUV transfers for seamless and comfortable travel. Certain premium resorts also offer exclusive water taxi or monorail services within their property networks.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Explore the diverse food trucks often found along International Drive or discover local, casual eateries in the Kissimmee and John Young Parkway areas. Many ethnic markets and specialty grocery stores offer excellent prepared foods.
Mid-range: The 'Restaurant Row' on Sand Lake Road in Dr. Phillips offers a vibrant collection of popular bistros and mid-to-upscale dining. Disney Springs and Universal CityWalk also boast numerous acclaimed restaurants with lively atmospheres and diverse menus.
Luxury: Indulge in exclusive dining experiences at Orlando's Michelin Guide-recognized establishments, chef-driven restaurants in Winter Park's Park Avenue, or the private dining rooms within premier resort hotels, offering tasting menus and impeccable service.
