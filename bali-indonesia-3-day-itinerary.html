Bali rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Bali rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Ubud's Cultural Heart & Verdant Retreats

09:00 AM

Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

Begin your day by exploring the ancient temples and lush forest home to over 1000 playful macaques. A spiritual and immersive experience.

11:30 AM

Tegalalang Rice Terraces & Iconic Swing

Marvel at the breathtaking emerald-green rice paddies carved into hillsides. Enjoy panoramic views and capture iconic photos, perhaps even try a thrilling jungle swing.

01:30 PM

Gourmet Lunch with Jungle Views

Indulge in a luxurious lunch at an upscale restaurant overlooking the river valley or rice fields, offering sophisticated Balinese fusion cuisine.

03:30 PM

Balinese Cooking Class or Art Workshop

Immerse yourself in Balinese culture with a private cooking class learning traditional recipes, or express your creativity in a batik or wood carving workshop.

07:00 PM

Traditional Balinese Dance & Fine Dining

Experience the enchanting Kecak or Legong dance performance in a beautiful temple setting, followed by an exquisite fine dining experience in central Ubud.

Day 2 Southern Coastline Bliss & Iconic Sunsets

09:00 AM

Private Yoga & Wellness Session

Start your day with a rejuvenating private yoga or meditation session at a cliffside retreat or luxury resort, overlooking the Indian Ocean.

11:00 AM

Uluwatu Temple & Cliff Views

Visit the majestic Uluwatu Temple, perched atop a steep cliff 70 meters above the sea. Admire the stunning ocean panoramas and intricate Balinese architecture.

01:00 PM

Cliffside Gourmet Lunch

Enjoy a luxurious lunch at an exclusive restaurant offering unparalleled views of the ocean, specializing in fresh seafood and international cuisine.

03:00 PM

Relax at a High-End Beach Club or Private Beach

Unwind and soak up the sun at an exclusive beach club in Uluwatu or Seminyak, with infinity pools, direct beach access, and premium service.

06:00 PM

Sunset Cocktails & Jimbaran Seafood Dinner

Witness a magical Balinese sunset with premium cocktails at a chic lounge, followed by a romantic fresh seafood dinner on the sands of Jimbaran Bay.

Day 3 Chic Lifestyle, Indulgence & Departure

09:30 AM

Luxury Spa Treatment

Indulge in a world-class Balinese spa treatment at a top-tier wellness center in Seminyak or Canggu, featuring traditional massages and holistic therapies.

11:30 AM

Boutique Shopping in Seminyak

Explore the stylish boutiques, art galleries, and designer stores in Seminyak, known for its unique fashion, jewelry, and homeware.

01:30 PM

Trendy Cafe Lunch in Canggu

Enjoy a delicious and healthy lunch at one of Canggu's renowned cafes, famous for their vibrant atmosphere, organic ingredients, and innovative menus.

03:00 PM

Relaxation or Art Gallery Exploration

Spend your afternoon relaxing by your private villa pool or exploring more of Bali's vibrant art scene by visiting contemporary galleries in the Kerobokan area.

06:30 PM

Farewell Fine Dining Experience

Savor a memorable farewell dinner at one of Bali's most acclaimed fine dining establishments, known for its exquisite cuisine and impeccable service, before heading to the airport.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Bali works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: Kuta, Legian, selected areas of Canggu - known for hostels, budget guesthouses, and guesthouses popular with backpackers and surfers.
Mid-range: Seminyak, Echo Beach area in Canggu, Sanur, central Ubud - offering boutique hotels, trendy resorts, and comfortable mid-range accommodations.
Luxury: Uluwatu (cliffside resorts), Nusa Dua (exclusive enclaves), high-end areas of Ubud (private villas with jungle views), Jimbaran (luxury beachfront properties) - suited for 5-star premier travel and ultimate indulgence.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: Ride-hailing apps for scooters (Gojek/Grab Moto) are the most common and cheapest. Limited public shuttle buses connect major tourist areas, but require good planning. Local Bemos are largely phased out for tourists.
Mid-range: Ride-hailing apps for cars (Gojek/Grab Car) offer convenience. Reputable metered taxis (e.g., Blue Bird) are widely available. Private car hire with a local driver for day trips provides flexibility and local insight.
Luxury: Pre-arranged luxury SUV transfers, specialized chauffeured services through hotels or dedicated tour operators, and even private helicopter transfers for scenic routes or quick island hops.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Local warungs (small eateries) scattered across the island, especially in Kuta, Legian, and local neighborhoods in Ubud. Markets like Pasar Badung (Denpasar) or morning markets in smaller towns offer authentic and inexpensive local delicacies.
Mid-range: Trendy cafes and bistros in Seminyak, Canggu, and central Ubud. Casual beach clubs with dining options along the coast. Popular international and local restaurants offering diverse menus in vibrant dining alleys.
Luxury: The fine dining scene in Seminyak, high-end cliff-edge restaurants in Uluwatu, exclusive resort dining experiences in Nusa Dua, and gourmet establishments tucked away in Ubud's lush landscapes. Look for tasting menus and chef's table experiences.
