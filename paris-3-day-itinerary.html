3 Days in Paris: A Complete Day-by-Day Itinerary

Paris rewards a plan. With only three days, the difference between a rushed, exhausting trip and a memorable one comes down to grouping the city by neighbourhood so you spend time seeing Paris rather than crossing it. This itinerary does exactly that: world-class art on Day 1, bohemian Montmartre and the Eiffel Tower on Day 2, and the royal splendour of Versailles on Day 3 — with options for every budget at the end.

Day 1 Grandeur, Art & Riverside Elegance The Louvre, the Seine, and a Saint-Germain evening

09:00 — Morning

The Louvre Museum

Begin your Parisian adventure with a pre-booked, timed-entry visit to the world-renowned Louvre. Focus on the key masterpieces — the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo and the Winged Victory of Samothrace — and allow at least three to four hours to take in its vast collections. A private guide is worth considering if you want a focused, in-depth tour rather than wandering the halls.

13:30 — Afternoon

Along the Seine

After the Louvre, step out onto the riverbanks. The stretch of the Seine around the Tuileries and Île de la Cité is made for an unhurried walk — bridges, bouquinistes and grand façades at every turn. It is the gentlest, most scenic way to reset between the morning's art and the evening ahead.

21:00 — Evening

Gourmet bistro dining in Saint-Germain-des-Prés

Conclude the day with dinner in the heart of Saint-Germain-des-Prés. This historic intellectual district is known for its charming, high-quality bistros. Seek out a restaurant serving classic French cuisine with a contemporary twist, paired with a well-chosen wine list.

Day 2 Bohemian Rhythms, Grand Views & Evening Romance Montmartre by day, the Eiffel Tower by night

09:30 — Morning

Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur

Spend the morning climbing the artistic streets of Montmartre, the village-within-a-city that drew Picasso, Dalí and Van Gogh. Wind up to the Sacré-Cœur Basilica for one of the best free panoramas in Paris, then lose yourself in the lanes around Place du Tertre where painters still work in the open air.

19:30 — Evening

Eiffel Tower illumination & a refined dinner

Experience the magic of the Eiffel Tower after dark. Pre-book tickets to ascend to the summit for breathtaking evening views, or stop at the second floor for a more intimate experience. Afterwards, find a refined dinner in the 7th arrondissement — ideally with a view of the sparkling tower, or in a sophisticated restaurant nearby known for French classics.

Day 3 Royal Opulence, Garden Serenity & Intellectual Heritage A day at the Palace of Versailles

09:00 — Full day

The Palace of Versailles

Dedicate your final day to the majesty of Versailles, an easy RER ride from the city. Pre-book a timed entry to walk the Hall of Mirrors and the royal apartments, then give yourself unhurried time in the gardens — the fountains, the Grand Canal and the Trianon estate reward those who linger. It is the perfect grand finale to three days in Paris.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Paris works on every budget — the trick is matching your neighbourhood and habits to your spend. Here's how the city breaks down across three tiers.

🛏 Where to stay

Budget: Goutte d'Or (18th), Belleville (20th) and Canal Saint-Martin (10th) offer character and value a little outside the centre.
Mid-range: Le Marais (3rd/4th), Saint-Germain-des-Prés (6th) and the Latin Quarter (5th) — central, walkable and full of life.
Luxury: The 1st (Louvre/Tuileries), 8th (Champs-Élysées/Madeleine) and 7th (Eiffel Tower/Invalides) for the grandest addresses.

🚇 Getting around

Budget: The extensive Métro (a Carnet of 10 tickets saves money), city buses and the RER for regional trips like Versailles. A Navigo Découverte pass pays off for a full week.
Mid-range: Mix Métro and RER for speed, top up with ride-share (G7 or Uber) late at night, and use Vélib' bike-share for scenic short hops.
Luxury: Private chauffeur or black-car hire for transfers, premium taxis, and even a river-taxi for a uniquely scenic transfer.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Rue Montorgueil (1st/2nd) for market finds and casual eateries, Marché des Enfants Rouges (3rd) for diverse food stalls, and the street-food vendors around Belleville and Ménilmontant for authentic, inexpensive bites.
Mid-range: The bistro alleys of the Latin Quarter (5th) and Saint-Germain (6th), hidden gems in Le Marais (3rd/4th), and the lively scene around Oberkampf (11th).
Luxury: The 8th (Champs-Élysées/Madeleine) and 1st (Place Vendôme) for Michelin-starred rooms, the 7th for elegant dining with views, and the Marais for boutique gastronomy.

Paris in 3 days: common questions

Is 3 days enough to see Paris?

Yes — three days covers the major highlights (the Louvre, the Eiffel Tower, Montmartre and a Versailles day trip) as long as you group attractions by area to cut down on travel time, which this itinerary does.

How much does 3 days in Paris cost?

Plan on roughly $203 per day for a budget trip, about $450 mid-range, and around $990 for a luxury day with five-star hotels and fine dining.

What's the best area to stay for first-time visitors?

Le Marais (3rd/4th), Saint-Germain-des-Prés (6th) or the Latin Quarter (5th) give you a central, walkable base. Budget travellers can look at the 10th, 18th or 20th; luxury stays cluster in the 1st, 7th and 8th.

How do you get around Paris?

The Métro is fastest and cheapest — a Carnet of 10 tickets suits short stays, a Navigo pass a full week. The RER links the city to Versailles and both airports.
