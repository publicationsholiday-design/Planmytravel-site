3 Days in Las Vegas: A Complete Day-by-Day Itinerary

Exactly how to spend a perfect 3-day trip to Las Vegas — what to see, where to stay, and how to get around.

Duration

Las Vegas rewards a plan. This 3-day itinerary groups the city by area so you spend your time seeing Las Vegas rather than crossing it — with a complete day-by-day schedule below, plus where to stay, eat and get around for every budget.

Day 1 Grand Arrival & Iconic Strip Wonders

03:00 PM

Check-in & Settle into Luxury

Arrive at your premium resort on the Strip, check in, and take time to unpack and familiarize yourself with the opulent surroundings. Enjoy the amenities, perhaps a welcome drink, and prepare for an evening of iconic experiences.

05:00 PM

Fountains of Bellagio & Conservatory Gardens

Experience the majestic Fountains of Bellagio show, a captivating dance of water and light. Afterwards, explore the stunning seasonal displays at the Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Gardens, a vibrant oasis of floral artistry.

07:30 PM

Gourmet Dinner with a View

Indulge in a premium dinner at a restaurant offering exquisite cuisine and panoramic views of the Strip, such as a high-floor dining experience at the Eiffel Tower Restaurant or a culinary journey at one of the upscale establishments overlooking the Bellagio fountains.

09:30 PM

World-Class Entertainment Show

Attend a breathtaking Cirque du Soleil show (e.g., 'O' at Bellagio or 'Mystere' at Treasure Island) or another highly acclaimed production that showcases the pinnacle of Las Vegas entertainment, complete with acrobatics, music, and dazzling visuals.

11:30 PM

Sophisticated Lounge Experience

Conclude your first day with a sophisticated drink at an exclusive lounge or cocktail bar within your resort or a nearby high-end property, soaking in the vibrant atmosphere and reflecting on the day's grandeur.

Day 2 Indulgence, Art & Thrills

10:00 AM

Gourmet Brunch Experience

Start your day with an indulgent brunch at a highly-rated restaurant, perhaps featuring unlimited mimosas and a vast selection of exquisite dishes, perfect for a leisurely morning.

12:00 PM

Luxury Resort Pool & Spa Relaxation

Spend the afternoon unwinding at one of Las Vegas's award-winning resort pools, complete with private cabanas, attentive service, and refreshing beverages. Consider adding a rejuvenating spa treatment for ultimate relaxation.

03:30 PM

High-End Shopping & Architectural Wonders

Explore the exclusive boutiques and luxury brands at destinations like The Forum Shops at Caesars Palace or Via Bellagio. Marvel at the intricate Roman-inspired architecture and unique design elements of these shopping destinations.

07:30 PM

Michelin-Starred Dining Experience

Savor an unforgettable dinner at a Michelin-starred or equivalent fine dining restaurant, known for its innovative cuisine, impeccable service, and sophisticated ambiance. Options abound at resorts like Wynn, Bellagio, or The Venetian.

10:00 PM

Nightlife & Rooftop Views

Experience Las Vegas nightlife at a sophisticated rooftop bar or a high-energy nightclub, offering stunning panoramic views of the city lights and a vibrant atmosphere. Enjoy signature cocktails and possibly live music or a top DJ.

Day 3 Cultural Gems & Grand Farewell

10:00 AM

Leisurely Breakfast & Art Exploration

Enjoy a relaxed breakfast at a charming cafe within your resort or a nearby spot. Afterwards, visit the Bellagio Gallery of Fine Art or explore the public art installations found throughout the CityCenter complex.

12:00 PM

Mob Museum Immersion (Downtown)

Venture to Downtown Las Vegas to explore The Mob Museum, a world-class institution dedicated to organized crime and law enforcement history. It offers a fascinating and immersive look into a critical part of American and Las Vegas history.

02:30 PM

Fremont Street Experience & Vintage Vegas Charm

After the Mob Museum, take some time to experience the Fremont Street Experience. Witness the Viva Vision light show, enjoy the street performers, and soak in the vibrant, classic Vegas atmosphere of downtown.

05:00 PM

Relaxation or Last-Minute Souvenirs

Return to the Strip for some downtime before dinner. You can relax by the pool, enjoy a final cocktail at a favorite bar, or browse for high-end souvenirs and gifts to commemorate your trip.

07:30 PM

Unforgettable Farewell Dinner

Conclude your Las Vegas journey with a memorable farewell dinner at a highly-acclaimed restaurant of your choice, perhaps one you've been wanting to try or a return to a favorite, ensuring a perfect end to your luxurious getaway.

Where to stay, eat & get around

Las Vegas works on every budget. Here is how it breaks down across three tiers.

🛌 Where to stay

Budget: Downtown Las Vegas (Fremont Street area), neighborhoods slightly south of the main Strip near Tropicana Avenue, or independent motels west of I-15 offer more affordable accommodation options with local charm.
Mid-range: Mid-Strip properties such as those around Flamingo Road, Harmon Avenue, or sections of Convention Center Drive provide a balance of central access and modern amenities. Look for trendy boutique hotels slightly off the main thoroughfare.
Luxury: The CityCenter complex (e.g., Aria, Waldorf Astoria, Vdara), the northern end of the Strip (e.g., Wynn, Encore, The Venetian, The Palazzo), and select resorts known for their elevated service and exclusive facilities represent the ultra-luxury zones.

🚉 Getting around

Budget: The RTC Deuce bus system offers affordable 24/7 service along the Strip and to Downtown. A 24-hour or multi-day pass is cost-effective. Walking is efficient for exploring segments of the Strip, especially for short distances between adjacent resorts.
Mid-range: Ridesharing services (Uber/Lyft) are widely available and convenient for navigating between resorts or to off-Strip locations. The Las Vegas Monorail provides quick access to several east-side Strip resorts and the Convention Center.
Luxury: Private car services, chauffeured limousines, and premium luxury rideshare options (e.g., Uber Black, Lyft Lux) offer a seamless and exclusive way to travel, ideal for airport transfers, evening engagements, and a heightened sense of comfort.

🍽 Where to eat

Budget: Casual eateries within resort food courts, specific discount dining spots on Fremont Street, and local taquerias or pho houses slightly off the Strip (e.g., Spring Mountain Road) provide excellent value. Look for happy hour specials at many restaurants.
Mid-range: Vibrant bistro alleys within resorts, popular steakhouses, and diverse international dining options found along the main Strip and in accessible off-Strip neighborhoods (e.g., Chinatown Plaza) cater to mid-tier preferences.
Luxury: Exclusive reservation-only establishments within properties like Bellagio, Wynn, Caesars Palace, and The Venetian, featuring Michelin-starred chefs and world-renowned culinary experiences, define the luxury dining scene.
